## The "I Have No Body Image" Surprise

Something that catches people off guard:

**The weight comes off fast on GLP-1.** Your mind doesn't adjust as quickly as your body does.

**You see a different size in the mirror. Your brain still expects the old one.**

This is normal. This is expected. This... passes.

Give it time. Your brain catches up to your body eventually.

## The "Food = Love" Confusion

Most of us have emotional connections to food:
- Comfort when stressed
- Celebration
- Bonding with friends
- Habit

On GLP-1, these patterns break. You're eating less, so old connections feel... different.

**You might feel:**
- Less comfort from food (because you're eating less)
- Less excited celebrating with food
- Less connected to people through shared meals

**This isn't a problem.** This is an opportunity.

## The "I Feel Less Like Me" Feeling

Some people on GLP-1 feel:
- Less energetic (even if they're objectively healthier)
- Less present in social situations involving food
- Less sure of their identity

**Reality check:** You're not "less like yourself." You're the YOU without the overeating. Without the food distraction.

That's different. But different isn't worse.

The core of who you are ‚Äî your values, your humor, your relationships, your purpose ‚Äî none of that has changed.

You're the same person making different choices about food.

## The "I'll Put It Back On" Anxiety

Common fear: "If I stop the medication, I'll gain it all back."

Reality: Maybe. Maybe not.

**But focusing on that anxiety instead of enjoying who you are NOW? That's not serving you.**

Cross that bridge when/if you get there.

The healthier behavior patterns you're building now ‚Äî protein first, eating less, portion awareness ‚Äî those can transfer to your post-medication life if and when that day comes.

**Don't let future anxiety ruin present happiness.**

## The Bottom Line

**GLP-1 medications are a tool, not a personality.**

You're not losing yourself by taking them. You're gaining a different relationship with food, your body, and appetite.

That relationship can be healthier.

That's a win.

Be kind to yourself during this transition. You're not required to have it all figured out.

**Progress, not perfection. Always.**
